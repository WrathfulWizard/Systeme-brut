# Ashbound — World & Lore

> The world ended quietly. Not in fire, but in *forgetting*. This is the bible
> for Ashbound's setting, regions, factions, and the loop the player lives in.

## The Premise

The world is held together by a single dying flame — the **Last Ember**. While
it burned, the dead stayed dead and the living remembered who they were. As it
guttered, both came undone: corpses rose without memory, and pilgrims who walked
toward the light lost their names along the road.

You are a **Penitent** — one of the nameless who woke in a graveyard with a
sword you don't remember carrying and a debt you don't remember owing. Something
in you refuses to forget. That refusal is the only thing the Ember answers to.

The journey is a pilgrimage to the **Cinderhold**, where the **Cinder Lord**
hoards the Ember's last warmth. To take the flame is to decide what the world
remembers — or whether it remembers at all.

## The Loop (why you keep coming back)

Embers scattered across the land are fragments of the Last Flame. **Tend an
ember** (a bonfire) and it pulls your scattered self back together: wounds close,
your flasks refill, and the hollow dead are drawn back to their posts.

When you fall, the **runes** you carried — the remembered weight of everything
you've killed — spill onto the ground as a stain of pale-magenta light. Reach it
again and you reclaim what was yours. Fall twice, and that memory is gone for
good. This is the bargain of the Ashbound: nothing is kept that isn't earned
twice.

## The Five Regions

The land is a broken wheel of five regions, each a different way of forgetting.

### 1. Ashen Barrows — *"where the first of us were buried"*
A field of cairns under a colourless sky, and the first place you wake. The dead
here are **Hollows**: slow, patient, and still going through the motions of lives
they no longer recall. The safest soil in a world with none.

### 2. The Mireheart — *"drowned, and dreaming still"*
Black water without a floor, south of the Barrows. The pilgrims who waded in
became **Mirelings** — fast, low, drawn to warmth. The Mire is said to *dream*,
and everything that moves in it is part of the dream.

### 3. Emberreach Highlands — *"the forge that would not die"*
Basalt terraces that bleed fire to the east. The smiths who cooled the world
beat their last blades here and never stopped; their armoured husks —
**Emberknights** — still guard the heat. Lava runs in the low channels: it does
not care about your dodge.

### 4. The Palewood — *"lanterns that were never lit by hands"*
A forest in the north lit from within by pale fungus and the glow of the lost.
The trees lean toward anything warm. The lights between the trunks were once
people who walked in and stayed.

### 5. The Cinderhold — *"the last throne, and what sits upon it"*
A fortress of black flagstone at the world's far edge, sealed by a wall of fog.
Beyond it waits the **Cinder Lord**, a colossus of ember-lit plate with the
world's final flame burning in his chest. End here, or end everywhere.

## Bestiary

| Foe          | Region(s)            | Read                                                  |
| ------------ | -------------------- | ----------------------------------------------------- |
| Hollow       | Barrows, Cinderhold  | Long, obvious wind-up. Punish the recovery.           |
| Mireling     | Mireheart, Palewood  | Fast, fragile, swarms. Spacing beats it; greed dies.  |
| Emberknight  | Highlands, Cinderhold| Heavy and slow but hits like a hammer. Trade nothing. |
| **Cinder Lord** | Cinderhold (boss) | Telegraphed overhead slams; at half-health the flame rises and everything speeds up. |

Every foe carries **runes** — the weight of a remembered death — and yields them
when it falls.

## The Penitent's Kit

- **A sword you do not remember.** Committed swings: once you start, you finish.
- **The dodge.** Brief invulnerability rolling *through* a blow, not away from it.
- **Stamina.** Memory is effort. Spend it on blades and rolls; it returns when
  you breathe.
- **Estus** — flasks of ember-light. Limited, refilled only at a tended ember.
- **Runes & Reinforcement.** Spend remembered deaths at an ember to *reinforce*
  yourself — to remember a little more of who you were, and grow stronger for it.

## Tone

Grief, not gore. Quiet, not grimdark-for-its-own-sake. The horror is **gentle
and total**: a world politely forgetting itself, and one small figure who won't.
Colour is rationed — each region grants itself one accent, and the only true
alarm in the world is the magenta light of a thing about to strike, or the
runes you left behind.
